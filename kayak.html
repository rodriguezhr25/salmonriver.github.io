Kayaking and rafting

Kayaking is a water sport, in which you sit facing the front (bow), in the direction of travel. It is paddled in a kayak, and achieves the displacement in the water (propel) with the help of the paddle.

The kayak is a relatively light boat made of plastic, fiberglass, kevlar, etc. The paddle of a kayak is double-bladed, and is not fixed on the kayak as it is usually in the sport of rowing.

Rafting consists of descending a river aboard a rowing boat. The oars are used to level and direct the raft or canoe while the descent occurs, which involves various difficulties (the rocks that appear on the road, the turbulence caused by the current and the unevenness, etc.).

The "descent" is literal: rafting usually begins at a certain height, with rivers whose channels run from the top to a lower part. This unevenness makes the boat glide over the water at high speed, making rafting an activity of some risk.

For rafting, it is essential to use a life jacket and helmet. On the boat, on the other hand, there must be ropes to carry out rescues, a whistle and a first-aid kit, among other items.
